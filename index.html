⛽ Tank size
How many litres you fill up in one visit. Used to calculate how much you save on fuel at a cheaper station. Most family cars hold 50–70L, smaller cars 35–45L.
litres
🚗 Fuel economy
Your car's miles per gallon. Used to calculate the cost of driving further to reach a cheaper station. Most petrol cars average 30–45 MPG. Check your trip computer or car spec sheet. A higher MPG means driving further costs you less.
MPG
⭐ Reference station
The station everything is compared against when calculating savings. Defaults to your nearest station. Tap ☆ on any result to set it as your personal reference — useful if you always use a particular station. Savings on each card show how much better or worse each alternative is vs this reference.
📐 How savings work
Net saving = fuel saving − extra drive cost

Example: reference 149p, target 146p, 40L tank, 40MPG, 2 extra miles round trip:
Fuel saving: 3p × 40L ÷ 100 = £1.20
Drive cost: 2mi ÷ 40MPG × 4.55 × 146p ÷ 100 = £0.33
Net = £1.20 − £0.33 = £0.87 → Worth it ✓
